TARLAC PROJECTS

WELCOME TO TARLAC

Tarlac is the most multicultural of the Central Luzon provinces. A mixture of four distinct groups – the Pampangos, Ilocanos, Pangasinenses and Tagalogs – share this province and living together. They have researched and developed about Sweet Potato R&D Center, Solar Powered Innovative Cart, and Food Safety Control System for Street Food and Ambulant Vendors.
Sweet Potato R&D Center
The project aims to strengthen, increase and sustain the Sweet Potato-Clean Planting Material (SP-CPM) production efficiency and effectivity through science and technology and precision agriculture interventions. The project involves 3 components: Optimization of SP-CPM production protocol; Establishment of spatial information in Central Luzon; and Integrated crop management.
Project MIST (Municipal Innovation through Science and Technology)
Food Safety Control System for Street Food and Ambulant Vendors
The project aims to establish Food Safety Control System Pilot Model through research-based assessment among selected food service establishments and ambulant street food vendors of Tarlac City. Through Tarlac State University’s Food Technology Department, profiling, environmental scanning and product sampling among respondents were conducted. The activities resulted to reliable data and information which will serve as basis for factual decision approach among policy making bodies of the LGU of Tarlac City towards its mandate of protecting the consumers’ health and welfare.
