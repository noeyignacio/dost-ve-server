WELCOME TO BATAAN

Bataan is an amazing historical and cultural region. Until recently, Bataan has developed projects like the Additive Manufacturing Research Laboratory, Automated Guide-Way Transit, and Balanga City National Science High School.
Additive Manufacturing Research Laboratory (AMReL)
AMREL is the first additive manufacturing research laboratory in the Philippines and since July 2, 2018, AMREL has been utilized to conduct research for undergraduate and graduate theses. Prof. John Ryan Dizon, project leader of AMREL, proudly shared these studies on the applications of 3D printing for defense applications and health care. He was also able to do technology demonstration with junior high school students of the Bataan National High School who then successfully created a 3D printed drone.

“We are launching two centers for 3D printing research and development and we, in DOST-PCIEERD, are privileged to be part of this game-changing initiative. The additive manufacturing research industry will open the doors to previously unimaginable possibilities, and every single 3D-printed product will unfold more innovations. Soon, how we create things will be different from what we’re used to,”
-Dr. Enrico Paringit | DOST-PCIEERD Executive Director

Adoption of DOST-SEI Designed 21st Century Classroom for Balanga City National Science High School

City Mayor Francis Anthony S. Garcia received P2-million worth cheque from DOST-Bataan Director Ms. Catalina V. De Leon-Cruz for an approved project that will enable the Balanga City National Science High School to be the first school in Bataan province to adopt and establish the 21st Century Classroom designed by DOST-Science Education Institute. The 21st Century Classroom promotes collaborative teaching and learning to support student-centered learning in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM).
