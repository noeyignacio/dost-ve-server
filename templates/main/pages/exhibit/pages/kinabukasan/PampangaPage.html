PAMPANGA PROJECTS

WELCOME TO PAMPANGA

Pampanga holds the title of being the Culinary Capital of the Philippines as they offer some of the best Filipino dishes, but there's more to this Central Luzon province that just good food. Pampanga started its first startup hub or technology business incubation at Holy Angel University, and its tamarind research and development center at Pampanga State Agricultural University.
Encephalon Holy Angel University’s Technology Business Incubator(TBI)
Encephalon Holy Angel University’s Technology Business Incubator (TBI) Encephalon HAU-TBI is one of Holy Angel University’s paramount projects. It is the University’s Facility for Incubation, Acceleration, and Intellectual Property (IP) generation of start-up projects. The actualization of this facility will help Holy Angel University to implement its fifth strategic initiative: Research, Development and Commercialization of Technology. In so doing, it will fulfil its strategic objective to be an authentic instrument for countryside development.

The DOST - Philippine Council for Industry, Energy and Emerging Technology Research and Development (PCIEERD) and the University of the Philippines, through the National Engineering Center, launched the Higher Education Institution Readiness for Innovation and Technopreneurship (HeIRIT) Program, a 12-month preparatory program for starting Technology Business Incubators (TBIs).
PSAU Tamarind Center
Pampanga State Agricultural University (PSAU) NICER Tamarind R&D Center The Pampanga State Agricultural University (PSAU) Tamarind RDE Center was established in 2010. The Center has generated one registered variety of sweet tamarind ‘Aglibut Sweet’. Processed products have been developed for commercialization. The Center has been quite successful in the commercialization of sweet tamarind at least at the regional level. Meanwhile, sour tamarind is an underutilized crop, but there has been an expanding demand by food processing companies for broth. This program is intended to expand the tamarind industry focusing on sour types in the Philippines. The present tamarind production system is fragmented and unorganized with many intermediaries from farm to market or to processing entities. Pampanga recorded the highest number of trees in Central Luzon, followed by Nueva Ecija. However, food ingredient companies require pods of at least three segments, no breakage and free from molds as quality standard. With the present traditional production system in tamarind households, S&T interventions is deemed necessary to expand the supply of quality pods to meet the quality and quantity requirements for small and medium processing entrepreneurs.
