High Value Crops Production Through HydroponicsTechnology

Project Details

As the worldâ€™s population continue to rise while agricultural land decreases food security and food safety is at risk. With commercial products prices are drastically changing over the years, and low income are generated by most of the families, healthy and good quality food has been put at the bottom of the priority bucket. The DOST-III in partmership with Central Luzon State University Center for Hydroponics and Aquaponics technology deployed twenty (20) total units of Hydroponics in the whole Central Luzon as response in the pursuit of sustainable development. One of the goals is to promote a sustainable technology for producing High Value Crops to ensure food safety and availability.

The DOST-III partnered with Local Government Units, High Schools, and workers groups or associations to promote sustainable, soil less, and modern agriculture. With this, the concept of soil-less agriculture was introduced and enable them to produce quality and safe food with higher productivity per unit area while using only a fraction of the water that they would otherwise consume under traditional farmer's practice.
In a one square meter of land area the group was able to plant 360 plants of high value crops mainly leafy vegetables using the technology and can harvest about 10 to 20 kg of leafy vegetables. The crops that can be planted using this technology are not limited to the following, pechay, mustard, lettuce, basil, mint, swamp cabbage, kale, and spinach.
