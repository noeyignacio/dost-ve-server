Samahang Zambaleña Sews Their Way to Success

Project Details

In 2016, 22 members of the Samahang Zambaleña Balincaguing Chapter thrive in their small sewing business. Their products include potholders, rags, cleopatra linen, and often curtains and pillowcase. The members only have 1 sewing machine and they often had difficulty in getting materials for their business. Because of this, some of the members quit and in 2018, only 5 members remain and were dedicated in continuing their sewing livelihood.

The group, through the coordination of LGU-San Felipe, sought the assistance of the Department of Science and Technology - III (DOST-III) for the acquisition of their livelihood needs. In 2019, the group was granted with the project that aims to upgrade their sewing facility through the provision of appropriate equipment such as high-speed sewing machines and edging machine. Over a year of implementation, the group was able to increase their sales and improve their productivity. Productivity in rags increased from 30pcs/day to 60pcs/day, potholder from 50pcs/day to 100pcs/day, and Cleopatra linen from 7pcs/day to 10pcs/day. With the Covid-19 pandemic, they were able to produce cloth facemasks and they cater bulk orders from different LGUs and other offices in Zambales. From 5 members operating the sewing facility, employment increased to 10. With the efforts of these members, they were able to increase their income up to 96% after 1 year of implementation.
