Improvement of Irrigation Practice through Solar Powered Pump

Project Details

Farmers from Central Luzon often have trouble watering their crops during the dry season. There is an increasing demand for irrigation due to the increasing demand for higher food production to sustain the continuously growing population of the country. High diesel and electricity costs affect the pumping requirements for irrigation of small and even big-time farmers. This problem is addressed by the DOST through the provision of Solar Powered Water Pump to different farmer associations scattered in Central Luzon.

One of the eleven beneficiaries of the Solar Powered Pump in Central Luzon; the Sta Cruz Farmers Association located in Sta Cruz, Lubao, Pampanga. The association primarily produces rice and corn. Through the DOST-III Community Empowerment through Science and Technology (CEST) Program they were provided with a Solar Powered Irrigation Pump. The Solar Powered Pump is used to irrigate their rice crops. Even during the dry season, the Solar Powered Pump in Sta. Cruz, Lubao, Pampanga can irrigate rice fields at a rate of a half-hectare per 4 to 6 hours.
