S&T driven livelihood towards empowerment of Abelling Tribe

Project Details

The municipality of San Jose in the Province of Tarlac is popularly known for its vast agroforestry area covering the mountains stretching up to the Zambales Region. Being a thick forested area, only 12% of its 59,282ha land area is dedicated for agricultural productivity. However, this does not hinder the people of San Jose to thrive on farming as a major source of income. Among these people are the Abelling Tribe which are currently residing at the hinterlands of San Jose. Aside from farming, the Abellings also thrive on livestock, hunting of wild animals, and foraging. As part of the five hundred indigenous people (IP) communities in the Philippines, the Abellings are also considered poor, most excluded, and disadvantaged sector in our society. With this, the Abellings have highly contributed to the poverty incidence of 24% of San Jose (highest record for Tarlac based on the 2012 PSA record).

The DOST-III, through its CEST program, has provided a package of S&T assistance to the Abelling community for them to become an active and sustainable community. Through the establishment of a root crop and vegetable processing facility, 24 Abelling tribe members were able to gain knowledge and skill in processing their root crops and vegetables to veggie polvoron, and powdered vegetable burger patties. They were also provided with equipment such as digital weighing scale, stainless steel working table, steamers, grinder machine, and double burner gas stove. In addition to this, the DOST-III, in partnership with Tarlac Agricultural University (TAU), provided assistance to the Abellings to develop products from their commodities such as karot, katmon, and amukaw. Although the product development is still on-going, TAU was able to produce samples of Katmon Jam, Katmon candy, Sinigang mix made from katmon, karot flour and chips, amukaw wine and vinegar.

DOST-III also provided a water filter technology to aid with the Abelling community in Sitio Agus & Sitio Tala in Brgy. Burgos that struggles for an access to potable water and avoid water-borne diseases. Five hundred forty-three Abelling tribe members benefit from the technology. No water borne disease incidence were reported from March to July 2020. Review sessions on Math and Science were also conducted in partnership with Tarlac State University for the DOST-SEI Undergraduate Scholarship Applicants in San Jose, Tarlac. This was participated by 57 Grade 12 students and a significant increase in the pre-test and post-test scores were recorded. This also increased the number of DOST-SEI undergraduate scholarship applicants from 15 applicants in 2018 to 62 applicants in 2019.
