Resilient Pantabangan: A Submerged Town Rises Above the Water

Pantabangan, a municipality in the province of Nueva Ecija in Central Luzon is the site of the Pantabangan Dam and Lake. Located in the northern part of Nueva Ecija, Pantabangan is situated at the foot of Mt. Mabilog below Mt. Dalimanok which are found between the Sierra Madre and Caraballo Mountain ranges. The construction of the Dam had great economic and social impact on the lives of Pantabangeños. About 8,100 hectares of productive farmlands and the town center along with the seven outlying barangays of Villarica, Liberty, Cadaclan, San Juan, Napon-Napon, Marikit and Conversion had to be submerged under the new lake to make way for the dam. Residents were relocated to higher ground overlooking the vast reservoir, which became the new Pantabangan town center. Despite the dam’s location in the municipality which generates hydro-electric power and irrigation water for Central Luzon, the poverty incidence in the area remains high. The 2012 poverty estimates of the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) reveal that Pantabangan has a 19.5% poverty incidence and ranks 7th among 31 municipalities in Nueva Ecija. Many of its barangays have become geographically isolated and therefore disadvantaged because of the dam’s construction.

Recognizing the urgent need to help some barangays in Pantabangan, DOST-III officially adopted the municipality as one of its 25 CEST-assisted communities through its regional program on the Expanded Implementation of Community Empowerment through Science and Technology (CEST) in Region III. To jumpstart the project, the CEST team initially collected socio-economic data about the municipality and its barangays to help identify the pressing issues and areas to be addressed using it as a backdrop to the conduct of the Rapid Rural Appraisal (RRA) in the community. To get a better assessment of the prevailing conditions in the areas, a multi-sectoral consultative dialogue participated by local/barangay leaders, farmer leaders, barangay health workers (BHWs), barangay nutrition scholars (BNS), and school principals and teachers. As an adjunct tool, the Technology Needs Assessment (TNA) instrument was also used to determine the technical needs of the community in the five (5) entry points of CEST.

Provision of livelihood opportunities such us cashew nut processing facilities and training on mushroom production for women's group and farmers group in barangay Conversion were provided. Also, CEST provides hydroponics and aquaponics technology to Ganduz National High School to promote production of high value crops through soilless agriculture. Empowerment on the health and nutrition of the community was also provided to the community, six (6) to thirty-six (6) months old children benefitted the 120 days feeding program. The feeding was participated by 35 malnourished and undernourished children, and 27 out of 35 or 77% return to normal nutritional status. The LGU Pantabangan was also provided with S and T intervention on the education and human resource development through the provision of Science and technology Research Based Openly Operated Kiosk and Upgrading of science laboratory facilities 6 schools benefitted these technologies. These interventions expected to help the students to increase their knowledge and interest on math and science subject. Also, an Interactive multimedia teaching aids technology was provided to IP schools in Conversion, Pantabangan, through this technology the student's participation in class increases and no number recorded of absenteeism among IP students while very few was noted to non-IP students. A review Class on math and science for DOST-SEI undergraduate scholarship applicants, 40 senior high school in Conversion was provided.

On the environmental protection and conservation entry points, CEST intervene a unit of Dual Drum Composter and Portable Biogas Digester. These technologies convert biodegradable waste into organic fertilizer and methane gas respectively. The Juan Time clock with Message Display Board was provided to LGU Pantabangan in response to the disaster risk reduction management. Synchronization of time pieces in the community was attained and the display board served as public information dissemination platform of the LGU where most of the announcement and weather bulletin forecasts were disseminated. With the CEST interventions, Pantabangeños were able to live happily despite their poverty-mired, isolated and disadvantaged position, lifting themselves above the challenges that had historically submerged their community. With the implementation of the CEST program in Pantabangan starting in 2018 and continuing to the present, the once submerged old community called of Pantabangan is beginning to rise up towards social and economic development.
