Carrageenan

Project Details

Carrageenan is a natural ingredient that comes from red seaweed (Irish moss - Chondrus Crispus). It is often used as an additive in different food making processes and food preservation. Through countless innovation and research, carrageenan, was further innovated through modified safe irradiation technology for the promotion of plant growth.

On the effectiveness of the Carrageenan Plant Growth Promoter (CPGP), the National Crop Protection Center (NCPC) under the leadership of Dr. Gil Magsino, claimed that the field applied with the technology was resistant to the tungro virus whilst the neighboring field have been observed to highly affected by the crop disease. For the yield performance, it has been found that the CPGP can increase rice yield for up to 25-30 percent. There are also researches contributing to the claims on greener and taller plants, more and taller tillers, and increase on the grain weight and quality.

After seeing the beneficial effects of using the CPGP during the SMART Hybrid Rice Production project in Central Luzon, the participating farmers are now buying on their own and are using the CPGP in their rice crop production.
