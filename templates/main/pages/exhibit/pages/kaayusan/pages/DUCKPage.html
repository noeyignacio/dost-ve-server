Design and Development of Disaster-Use Converted Ketch (D.U.C.K.)

By DHVSU, Bacolor, Pampanga

This research and development project aims to design and develop an amphibious vehicle intended to enhance disaster relief and response operation most especially during typhoons and floods. DUCK stands for Disaster-Use Converted Ketch, which is a transport system that can be used both inland and water. It will be designed according to the needs of the flood-affected areas in the province. DUCK can be used to rescue affected people and transport them to evacuation areas and to distribute relief goods even too hard to reach affected areas. DUCK will benefit the Pampanga Province and when proven efficient, it can be duplicated in other areas of the Region.

The design and simulator software specifically for buoyancy and loading capacity will be used in this research project. This DUCK will be readily available in times of severe weather especially in the presence of severe typhoons and floods. Typhoon and flood adaptation and mitigation using DUCK may be implemented in Pampanga after the completion, testing, and analysis of the prototype.
